narrative description of the [[Cyclopean ritual]] the [[Heroes]] perform, driven by [[Leon]]'s research into the [[cyclops scroll]] from Session 1 

### Phase I: The Offering of Memories

The scent of burning kindling mixes with the heavy, organic aroma of the crushed sheep’s heart, the sharp tang of dill, and salt. At the exact center of your circle lies the stone, resting over the offering. The silence is absolute.

One by one, you must approach the stone, touch its cold surface, and silently surrender your most significant memory—the anchor of who you are.

- **If a true memory is offered:** The stone drinks the thought, pulsing a deep, vibrant green.
    
- **If the offering is half-hearted or false:** The stone flashes an angry, jarring red. A cold, echoing voice rings directly inside your skull: _“Try again. Nothing is free.”_
    

Once the final, true memory is accepted, the stone shifts. It begins to glow with a pale, amber-yellow light, humming with a vibration you can feel in your teeth. In perfect sync, you step forward, join hands, and look down into the amber light.

---

### Phase II: The Trade and The Descent

Looking into the stone is like peering through a glass viewport at first. You see a city of sprawling grandeur—**Eldoni, the capital of Eldoria**. But the view is fleeting.

Suddenly, your mind experiences a violent tearing sensation. You feel a core piece of your own past being physically dragged out of your consciousness. In its place, a heavy, uninvited memory forces its way in. Around the edges of your mind, only a faint, frustrating wisp of what you used to know remains. You cannot remember the old memory; you only know the void it left behind.

The viewport shatters. You aren't looking down anymore. You are there.

---

### Phase III: The Vision (The Four Steps)

#### Step 1: The Ruin of Eldoni

The transition is instantaneous. You are standing in a major thoroughfare of Eldoni, but the capital is breaking. A choking violet static fills the sky, and the roar of collapsing stone is deafening. You feel the weight of phantom armor and the suffocating terror of a battle already lost.  You are the last surviving member of your battalion,  looking for the other members of your company to begin a final attempt.

The spires which adorn the outer walls are smoking, the inner walls are crumbled, and you know that everything stands on this last moment.  Yet, the most terrifying part is the atmosphere. The city feels completely empty of life—no living birds fly, no stray animals scurry through the debris. Instead, a thick, greasy malice hangs in the air, a menacing and undefined evil watching from the shadows. You know instinctively that a great shadow is here, though you cannot see it.

The vision shifts.  

Driven by panic and a sudden, burning need to find an answer, you all see through Leon’s determined eyes.  He views the city from a high balcony, then turns and sprints away from the fallen Eldoni, hurtling down the steps of a grand, crumbling tower. He descends into a subterranean library. As he runs, his eyes catch a massive, permanent mural on the wall: **Balfour I and Malazar, side-by-side, signing a fateful contract.**

Leon suddenly remembers he needs to find .... something... something to stop this nightmare. But _where is it?_ The moment his mind, all of your shared minds, focused on the need.  He steps once more down the hall, but this step whisks him away. The world warps around you. 

#### Step 2: The Hearth

The library stretches, blurs, and snaps like a rubber band.

Suddenly, the smell of smoke and ash is gone. The air is quiet, rich with the rich, inviting aroma of baking bread and slow-simmering broth. You are standing inside a cozy, deeply lived-in home. The details here are hyper-sharp and solid.

Your gaze is pulled immediately to the stone mantlepiece over the crackling fireplace. Resting there is an object—something small and handheld, something unassuming yet radiating a dormant resonance. You share a moment of precient realization. This object is part of the solution. But it isn't the only one. _Where is the rest?_  Your need drives the next step, and instead of walking to the hearth to learn more about this small object, another shift tears you from the scene.

#### Step 3: The Researcher's Records Room

The warm cottage walls dissolve outward into shadow.

Your feet are suddenly resting on old hardwood. The air is thick with the scent of decaying parchment and old ink. You are in a private records room.  As you glance at the closest open scroll, you sense a familiarity to the writing and speech you read.

Shelves and tables are crammed with massive stacks of scrolls and heavy, leather-bound books. Roughly half of the room is organized into meticulous, neat piles. The other half, however, is a chaotic blur of activity. Open tomes are scattered face-down, scrolls have been hastily unrolled and left askew, and ink jars sit uncapped alongside frantic, half-finished notes. The space bears the unmistakable mark of its owner having rifled through it in a state of sudden, desperate urgency.

Just then, the same malice and dread from the beginning of the vision comes over you.  The oppressive, unseen malice from the burning city presses against the boundaries of this room, a reminder that time is running out. You need the final piece. Knowing this worked to get you here, you think of where it must be hidden and step into the need.

The shadows twist, pulling you forward one last time.

#### Step 4: The Tech Facility

The frantic records room blurs into clean, oppressive lines of cold steel and glowing ley-lines.

You are deep in the bowels of an **arcane tech facility**, located in the distant southwest corner of Eldoni. The silence here is clinical and absolute. You are standing at the end of a long, narrow room.

At the far end, resting at the end of a thin, austere bed used for the long nights in research, is a heavily padlocked chest. It hums with a volatile, locked-away energy.

Looking at that chest, a deep, instinctual certainty cements itself into your minds: **This is one piece of a larger trinity.** If you face the final battle in the capital without breaking into this facility and retrieving what is inside that chest first, Eldoni will burn, and the entity in the shadows will win.  Mist begins to cover your vision and the shadow which you fear draws you in.

---

### Phase IV: The Wake

The vision snaps like a broken mirror.

With a violent, collective gasp, you are yanked backward. The sterile tech facility vanishes, replaced by the familiar crackle of your own campfires. You are back in the circle, still holding hands, your chests heaving for breath.

In the dead center, the meticulously placed rock has gone completely dark and cold. Beneath it, the mixture of dill, salt, and the sheep's heart has been utterly consumed, reduced to nothing but a fine, pale white ash.
