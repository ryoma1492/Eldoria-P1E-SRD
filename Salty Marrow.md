	 
[[Salty Marrow]] is a crewmate of [[The Silver Vulture]] and serves as an appointed inland guide for the [[Heroes]] following the Vulture’s Bargain at the wreckage of the [[Wreck Island]].

He is a maritime survivor of the outer curtain routes — a man who has spent too long between salvage law, pirate accords, and the creeping ecological collapse surrounding the [[Fool's Curtain]].

Despite his rough trade background, he does not present as cruel or predatory. Instead, he is unsettlingly calm about systemic decay, as if it is simply weather he has learned to sail through.

---

# Role in the Campaign

- Guide from sea salvage zones into inland Spire territories
- Interpret regional pirate salvage law
- Provide commentary on burial barges and Curtain activity
- Serve as narrative contrast between “economic pragmatism” and “cosmic horror”

---

# Personality

- Dry humor under pressure
- Calm acceptance of horrific systems
- Quietly observant
- Avoids direct moral judgment unless pushed
- Speaks as though catastrophe is routine bureaucracy

He does not deny suffering.

He simply refuses to be surprised by it.

---

# Appearance

- Weather-worn naval coat reinforced with stitched tarcloth
- Salt-crusted beard and brows
- One eye slightly clouded (old injury or exposure to Curtain mist)
- Carries salvage tags and chain tokens for legal claims

---

# Accent / Speech Style

Salty Marrow speaks with a **light Canadian cadence**, softened vowels, and understated phrasing.

He often sounds like he is:
- apologizing for reality itself
- mildly inconvenienced by apocalypse
- trying not to be dramatic about very dramatic things

---

# Dialogue Samples

## First Meeting (The Silver Vulture Bargain)

> “Right then… I’ll be honest with you folks, I’ve seen worse wrecks. Not many, mind you. But worse ones. Usually they’re still moving, which is… less pleasant.”

> “Name’s Marrow. Salty Marrow. Don’t ask about the first name, it’s not important and neither is my birth certificate anymore.”

> “We’re offering you a clean deal. Clean-ish. Clean enough that no one gets eaten by paperwork later, anyway.”

---

## On the Dragon-Gold Hoard

> “That gold’s… well, it’s not so much currency anymore as it is a kind of *emotional hazard*. Long exposure to dragon presence does that.”

> “You try to sell it, and people start wanting it in ways that are… not economically healthy.”

---

## On the Burial Barges

> “They call it passage. Real gentle word for it. Passage sounds like you’re going somewhere pleasant.”

> “Truth is, those barges don’t really *arrive* anywhere. They just… stop being a problem for whoever built the system.”

> “Which I suppose is one way to define spirituality. Not my preferred one, but I’m not writing the brochures.”

---

## On the [[Fool's Curtain]]

> “That thing out there? That’s not weather. It’s more like… accumulated decisions nobody wanted to keep track of anymore.”

> “You don’t sail through it. You get filed into it.”

---

## On the [[Savage Spire]]

> “Spire folk’ll tell you it’s sacred. Everything’s sacred if you say it enough times, I find.”

> “Me, I think it’s just very tall and very committed to not falling down yet.”

---

# Secrets (DM Only)

- Salty Marrow has previously guided groups that never returned from the Spire
- He is aware the Curtain is *structural*, not merely environmental
- He suspects the burial barges are part of a long-term ecological “load balancing” system
- He will quietly encourage the party toward truths, but never force revelation

---

# Hooks

- May reappear later as an intermediary between inland and maritime factions
- Could become morally compromised if the party pushes salvage law too far
- May eventually refuse service if [[Malazar]]-related corruption intensifies again
